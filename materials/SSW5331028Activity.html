SSW533 Guest Lecture Activity: Understanding Real-World Performance Issues

This activity helps you explore how real software engineers identify and fix performance issues in large open-source projects. Your feedback will help us refine this course to make future activities more useful and realistic.

General Instructions:

  • Your task is to analyze three issue tickets from the Apache Avro project to understand real-world performance issues.
  • You will act as a software quality analyst, exploring performance problems and other factors involved in fixing the issues.
  • For each ticket, you should:
  1. Determine whether it is performance related.
  2. If so, populate the fields in the provided template below.
  • This activity should take about 1 hour in total.

Survey & Research Participation

After completing this assignment, you are invited to:

Take a 5-minute anonymous survey about what you learned. This helps us understand which parts of the activity were most effective for learning performance engineering.

Complete a 1-minute consent form to allow us to include your anonymized submission in our analysis. This helps us improve course materials and publish findings on effective teaching practices.

These steps are completely voluntary.  However, your participation is greatly appreciated and helps us make this course better for future students.

Task Instructions:

Background: Apache Avro is a remote procedure call and data serialization framework developed within the Apache Hadoop project. It uses JSON for defining data types and protocols and serializes data in a compact binary format.

For each of the following tickets, follow the same analysis steps below. If the issue is not performance related, skip to the next task.

Each task takes approximately 10–20 minutes.
